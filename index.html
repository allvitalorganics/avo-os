Decision authority matrix — who decides what alone

Tier escalation prevents both decision paralysis and unauthorized commitments. Default to escalating higher when uncertain — the cost of over-consulting is low; the cost of discovering a decision wasn't made is high.

TierWho decidesWhat kind of call
Tier 1 — SoloFunction owner decides alone · logged afterward in standupDay-to-day operational calls within their function (e.g., Justin adjusts an ad budget · Alex replies to a press inquiry · Dawon picks a font weight)
Tier 2 — Two-founderFunction owner + one peer approvalCross-functional calls or commitments to outside parties (e.g., signing a 공구 organizer · accepting a press interview · approving an ad creative shift)
Tier 3 — All-handsDiscussed in weekly review · documentedStrategic shifts (e.g., adding a new channel · changing a pricing tier · pausing a campaign mid-flight)
Tier 4 — CEOAlex decides with team inputStrategic / capital decisions (e.g., raising · launching SKU 2 · signing exclusive supplier · entity formation)

Spending authority — by amount

AmountApproval needed
Under ₩500K (~$370)Function owner alone · logged in standup

₩5M – ₩25M (~$3,700 – $18,500)All-hands discussion in weekly review

The rule: if you're uncertain which tier a decision falls in, escalate up. Faster decisions made wrong cost more than slightly slower decisions made right.

Founder accountability — bi-weekly 1:1s

Alex + Justin 1:1, every two weeks, 30 min, no agenda required. Not optional. Founder departures are statistically common in CPG ventures — the 1:1 is the primary prevention mechanism. Surface tension before it compounds. The cadence prevents the need for the Co-Founder exit mechanics.

Cadence rule — Skip vs Post Weak

"Never miss a posted day" AND "Skip if it isn't ready" are NOT contradictory. Missing a scheduled day breaks audience trust. Posting a weak episode breaks quality. Solution: post late rather than weak. Move the day, don't deliver subpar content to keep the slot.

If an episode isn't ready by Mon 5pm KST: skip Monday, post Wednesday instead. Tell the audience nothing — they'd rather get a great Wednesday post than a weak Monday post.

Tease forward pattern — every episode plants the next

The closing 5-10 seconds of every Reel + final caption line teases the next episode. This creates narrative dependency — audience returns not because the post was great, but because they need to know what happens next. Examples:

Three financial layers — Revenue (vanity), Contribution margin (reality), Cash (truth)

Most operators look at revenue. Revenue is a vanity metric — it doesn't say if you're making money. The three layers below get tracked separately. Every weekly review reads all three:

LayerWhat it tells youHow to compute
1. Revenue (vanity)Top-of-funnel — how much money moved. Doesn't account for COGS, fees, refunds, ads.Sum of gross sales across all channels
2. Contribution margin (reality)What's actually left to fuel growth. The "do we have a business?" number.Revenue – COGS – shipping – payment processing – CAC for that order
3. Cash (truth)What's actually in the bank. Survives even when contribution looks good but cash flow is broken (settlement delays, refund spikes).Mercury / 재니스바스켓 통장 balance · checked daily
Rule: Revenue can be growing while contribution margin is collapsing (e.g., scaling paid ads at unprofitable CAC). Cash can be collapsing while revenue + contribution look fine (e.g., Coupang D+45 settlement delays compound). Track all three. Make decisions on contribution + cash. Never on revenue alone.
